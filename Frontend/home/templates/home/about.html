About MediaCat

MediaCAT started as a collaboration between an anthropologist, a computer scientist, and digital librarians. Alejandro Paz, a digitally-curious anthropologist, was doing manual searches to see how digital news sites around the world were picking up on the expanding Israeli online news in English. He knew his ethnographic project would be helped by some kind of digital tracking tool (which surely must exist—no?). Kirsta Stapelfeldt, the director of the new Digital Scholarship Unit at UTSC Library, thought the digital component had potential, and introduced it to Anya Tafliovich of the Computer and Mathematical Sciences Department. Anya was looking for interesting projects for her third year software engineering course in Fall 2014. Kim Pham, the Digital Projects & Technologies Librarian at UTSC, also came on board to help shepherd development. Helped along by a small seed grant from the Vice-Principal of Research Impact Fund, we hired students from Anya’s class to continue development during Summer and then Fall/Winter work studies in 2015/2016. We also hired anthropology students as work studies to help testing and to develop use case scenarios.
